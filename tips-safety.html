Kitchen Safety Tips

Stay safe in the kitchen with these tips, organized by category:

Burn & Heat Safety
Avoid burns and heat-related accidents.
  • Use oven mitts to prevent burns.
  • Keep pot and pan handles turned in to avoid knocking them off the stove.
  • Let hot pans cool before washing to prevent steam burns.
  • If children are cooking, make sure they are supervised at all times.
Knife Safety
Handle knives with care.
  • Store knives safely and children how to use them without causing any danger.
  • Use a sharp knife for safer, easier chopping.
  • Always cut away from your body and use a stable cutting board.
  • If you’re not a pro with the knife, make sure to cut slowly and carefully.
Food Safety
Keep your food safe to eat.
  • Keep raw meat and seafood separate from other foods.
  • Wash hands and surfaces often to prevent cross-contamination.
  • Cook foods to safe temperatures using a thermometer.
  • Refrigerate leftovers promptly to prevent spoilage.
  • Use clean utensils and cutting boards to avoid cross-contamination.
General Kitchen Safety
Tips for a safe cooking environment.
  • Clean spills immediately to prevent slips and falls.
  • Keep electrical cords away from water.
  • Use a step stool for reaching high shelves.
  • Never leave cooking food unattended.
  • Keep a fire extinguisher nearby and know how to use it.
  • Have a first aid kit accessible in case of emergencies.
  • Keep the kitchen tidy and free of clutter.
  • Wear an apron to protect your clothing.
  • Store heavy items on lower shelves to avoid injuries.
  • After cooking, turn off all appliances and clean up spills and utensils.
