Preparation Tips

The first step in cooking is preparation and making sure everything is ready before you start. Here are some tips to help you prep like a pro according to what you’re cooking:

Vegetable Prep
How to prepare vegetables for cooking.
  • Wash and dry all vegetables before cutting.
  • Peel and chop vegetables as needed for your recipe.
  • Use a sharp knife for even slices and safer handling.
  • Don’t use too much oil while sautéing to avoid sogginess and to keep it healthy.
Meat & Seafood Prep
Tips for prepping meat and seafood safely.
  • Keep raw meat and seafood separate from other foods.
  • Trim excess fat and remove bones if needed.
  • Pat meat dry before seasoning or cooking for better browning.
  • Don’t forget to marinate for extra flavor.
Baking Prep
Get ready for baking success.
  • Measure all ingredients accurately before starting.
  • Bring eggs and butter to room temperature for better mixing.
  • Preheat your oven and prepare pans with parchment or grease.
Grilling Prep
Set up for a great grilling experience.
  • Clean and oil the grill grates to prevent sticking.
  • Marinate meats ahead of time for extra flavor.
  • Have all tools and ingredients within reach before lighting the grill.
  • Don’t forget to preheat the grill for even cooking and don’t rush the process.
Fruit Prep
Tips for preparing fruit for cooking and baking or eating.
  • Wash all fruit thoroughly before use.
  • Peel and chop fruit as needed for your recipe.
  • Use a sharp knife for clean cuts and safer handling.
  • If fruit are to be eaten like that, use drinking water to wash them.
  • Consider using ripe fruits for a better taste.
Poultry Prep
Tips for preparing poultry for cooking.
  • Use a meat thermometer to ensure proper cooking temperatures.
  • Pat poultry dry before seasoning for better skin crisping.
  • Cook poultry to an internal temperature of 165°F (75°C).
  • Let poultry rest before carving to retain juices.
  • Make sure your poultry is well cooked and not raw in the center.
General Kitchen Prep
Tips for a smooth cooking experience.
  • Read your recipe all the way through before starting.
  • Gather all tools and ingredients in advance.
  • Clean as you go to keep your workspace organized.
  • Make sure your hands and utensils are clean.
  • Make sure your kitchen is tidy and clean your utensils after food preparation and set them for the next cooking session.
